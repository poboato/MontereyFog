The Science of Fog

MARINE LAYER Ocean Meets Air

What Actually Causes the Fog?

The Monterey Bay fog is technically a "marine layer" — cool, moist air from the Pacific Ocean meets warmer inland air and condenses into a thick blanket of fog that can reduce visibility to near zero. The phenomenon is most common in summer (yes, summer), when inland temperatures rise and the temperature contrast is greatest. This means that while your friends in Los Angeles are at the beach, you're wearing a parka in July. Welcome to Monterey.

FOG RECORDS For Bragging Rights

Fog Records and Achievements

CSUMB holds the unofficial CSU record for most foggy days per year. The campus has reportedly considered offering a minor in "Fog Appreciation Studies" (we made that up — but honestly, it would fill). The fog is so persistent that the university's original architectural plans reportedly included a "fog navigation system" that was just a really long rope from the parking lot to the library. The fog has its own Yelp page (2.5 stars — "too much atmosphere"). A 2025 survey found that 63% of students say the fog has "become a part of their identity." The other 37% couldn't be reached for comment — they were lost in it.

SURVIVAL Essential Skills

How to Survive the Fog: A Student's Guide

1. Always carry a jacket. Even in August. Especially in August. 2. Invest in a headlamp — the sun sets behind the fog at approximately 3pm in winter. 3. Learn to navigate by the glow of the library. 4. Accept that your hair will never be dry. 5. Embrace the damp. It's part of who you are now. 6. If you hear someone talking about "UV index," they are a transfer student. Be gentle with them. They'll learn.

PSYCHOLOGICAL Real Talk

The Five Stages of Fog Grief

1. Denial: "It'll burn off by noon." (It won't.)
2. Anger: "Why did I choose a coastal school? I could be at CSU Bakersfield right now."
3. Bargaining: "If I wear a bright enough jacket, maybe the sun will notice me."
4. Depression: "I haven't seen my shadow in months. I don't know what I look like in natural light."
5. Acceptance: "I am a creature of the mist. The fog is my home now. We Raft Up here."

Most students reach Stage 5 by mid-October of their first year. Transfer students typically take an extra semester. Out-of-state students from Arizona may never recover.
