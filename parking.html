Parking (An Educational Equity Issue)

PARKING 2023 — Present

Students Protest $500 Parking Permits as "Educational Equity Issue"; Permits Now $588

In 2023, 40 students marched demanding free parking, arguing that charging for parking on "stolen Indigenous land" was a racial justice issue. Parking Services responded by raising prices to $588/year, citing "state law." The university also notes that Lot B is "only a 15-minute walk from campus — in good weather. In fog, allow 45 minutes and a prayer."

PARKING Also Always

Parking Permit Prices Defy Inflation, Common Sense

At $588 for an annual pass, CSUMB parking permits cost more than a Monterey Bay Aquarium membership ($95), a wet suit for the dive program ($150), and a year of Verve Coffee at the Library Cafe ($540). "I've done the math," says senior Alex R. "Parking is the most expensive part of my education after tuition. I've considered just living in my car. Oh wait, I need a parking permit for that too."

"I came for the marine science program. I stayed because I parked in Lot B in 2023 and I'm still trying to find my car."

— Jordan K., Lot B, Section 7, Row Z

"I paid $588 for my parking pass and then my class was moved to Zoom. I now park in Lot B, sit in my car, and attend lectures from the driver's seat. I've made peace with my choices."

— Jamie L., the Lot B Zoomer

Alternatives to Driving (There Are a Few)

FREE Otter Cycle Center

Free Bike Rentals at the Otter Cycle Center

Located on the main campus, the Otter Cycle Center offers free bike rentals, free repairs, and group rides. It's the university's way of admitting that parking is hopeless while also promoting fitness. Students can borrow a bike for the semester, ride to class, and return it whenever — usually around the time they realize they've been parking in Lot B for three years.

PUBLIC MST Bus

Monterey-Salinas Transit — Free with Student ID

CSUMB students ride MST buses for free with their student ID. Buses run from campus to downtown Monterey, Seaside, Marina, and beyond. It's reliable, it's free, and it doesn't require a $588 permit. The only downside: the bus stop is a 10-minute walk from most classes. In the fog. But you were going to walk anyway.

🅿️ Pro tip: There is no pro tip. Parking here is a nightmare. Take the free bike from the Otter Cycle Center. Or the bus. Or accept the fog as your eternal companion and walk. We're all in this together.

Parking Permit Rates

  • Annual: $588 (a month of rent)
  • Academic year: $441 (slightly less painful)
  • Semester: $220.50 (bite-sized regret)
  • Daily: $2-5 (the "I'm feeling lucky" option)
  • Citation: $20-60 ("educational equity fee")

Alternatives to Driving

  • Otter Cycle Center: Free bikes (they work)
  • MST Bus: Free with student ID

  • Carpool: Make friends, share trauma
  • Acceptance: The final stage of parking grief
Real talk: The parking situation is bad. We know. The university knows. The state knows. Bring a bike. Or good shoes. Or a therapist.
