Welcome to the Fog (We Raft Up Here)

Congratulations on finding CSU Monterey Fog — the only university in America where "I can't see my future" is both a metaphor and a weather report. We're perched one mile from the Pacific Ocean on the bones of Fort Ord, a decommissioned Army base that the military decided was "too foggy" for training. We took that as a challenge.

Here, you'll find 61% of students are first-generation college-goers, our scientific diving program is the largest in the country, and the library is genuinely stunning — a $64 million cathedral of glass and concrete that one local paper called "architectural performance art." You can even eat and drink inside it, which is either progressive or desperate. Probably both.

🦦 Raft Up: Wild sea otters hold hands while sleeping so they don't drift apart. At CSUMB, we call this "Rafting Up." It's our whole deal. There's a 9-foot bronze otter statue on campus and students rub its nose before exams for good luck. This is real. This is who we are.

The Fogcast — News You Can Barely See Through

OTTERS BEING OTTERS April 2025

Campus Unveils 9-Foot Bronze Otter Statue; Students Immediately Start Rubbing Its Nose

The "Otters for Life" statue — two otters playing in bronze waves — debuted to a crowd of 500. President Quiñones declared it "full of magic" and told students to "touch the hand of the otter" if they're lost, and "touch the nose to pass your exams." Students now queue up to rub the nose before midterms. The statue has not yet filed a restraining order, which administration considers a win.

SERVICE LEARNING Ongoing

Required Community Service Somehow Doesn't Suck; Students Log 81,000 Hours Annually

CSUMB is one of the few universities in the country that requires service learning for graduation — 25+ hours of community work with 400+ local partners. Students partner with Save Our Shores, the Big Sur Land Trust, and local schools. "I was mad about it at first," says junior Maria Torres, "but then I spent a semester cleaning up the beach and realized I've been scammed into having a good time and helping the environment. I feel attacked."

GOOD STUFF Ongoing

Scientific Diving Program Quietly Becomes Largest in the United States

CSUMB's scientific diving program trained 300 divers per year — more than any university in the country. Students get weekly access to kelp forests, rocky intertidal zones, and the Monterey Submarine Canyon (the largest on the West Coast). Graduates have gone on to work at NOAA, the Monterey Bay Aquarium, and National Geographic. "I came here for the dive program," says alum Kalikonani Dailey. "I got a job. I'd recommend it — if you can find the campus through the fog."

COMMUNITY Dec 2025

Three Seaside Women Graduate Together at Ages 52, 62, and 64, Immediately Put CSUMB to Shame

Audrey Jones (62), Loyce Bryant (52), and Cynthia Matory (64) — non-traditional students from Seaside who bonded during their studies — graduated together. Jones pinned SEVEN stoles to her robe and said: "I went back to give back. I also want to be an example for my grandchildren." The three plan to launch a nonprofit. They are objectively cooler than everyone else.

YIKES² Feb 2023

University Encourages Students to Report Professors for "Race-Related Stress," Fox News Loses Its Mind

CSUMB's counseling center published a guide telling students to "think broadly about what could be an act of racism" — including professors "consistently not calling on" you. Fox News ran 47 segments. The guide is still online. The professor who doesn't call on you is still in his office, blissfully unaware.

FREE SPEECH Sept 2025

HCOM Lecturer Says Charlie Kirk's Assassination Wasn't That Sad; Receives Death Threats

Lecturer Cody Roberts posted an Instagram story saying he "couldn't muster much sympathy" after right-wing activist Charlie Kirk was assassinated. Libs of TikTok reposted it. Roberts was doxxed. The HCOM department got a threatening email saying "let's go." FIRE sent a letter defending his free speech. The administration released a statement that was 800 words long and said nothing. Roberts is still teaching.
