Welcome to the Fog (We Raft Up Here)

Congratulations on finding CSU Monterey Fog — the only university in America where "I can't see my future" is both a metaphor and a weather report. We're perched one mile from the Pacific Ocean on the bones of Fort Ord, a decommissioned Army base that the military decided was "too foggy" for training. We took that as a challenge.

Here, you'll find 61% of students are first-generation college-goers, our scientific diving program is the largest in the country, and the library is genuinely stunning — a $64 million cathedral of glass and concrete that one local paper called "architectural performance art." You can even eat and drink inside it, which is either progressive or desperate. Probably both.

🦦 Raft Up: Wild sea otters hold hands while sleeping so they don't drift apart. At CSUMB, we call this "Rafting Up." It's our whole deal. There's a 9-foot bronze otter statue on campus and students rub its nose before exams for good luck. This is real. This is who we are.

Campus Unveils 9-Foot Bronze Otter Statue; Students Immediately Start Rubbing Its Nose

SERVICE LEARNING Ongoing

Required Community Service Somehow Doesn't Suck; Students Log 81,000 Hours Annually

CSUMB is one of the few universities in the country that requires service learning for graduation — 25+ hours of community work with 400+ local partners. Students partner with Save Our Shores, the Big Sur Land Trust, and local schools. "I was mad about it at first," says junior Maria Torres, "but then I spent a semester cleaning up the beach and realized I've been scammed into having a good time and helping the environment. I feel attacked."

GOOD STUFF Ongoing

Scientific Diving Program Quietly Becomes Largest in the United States

CSUMB's scientific diving program trained 300 divers per year — more than any university in the country. Students get weekly access to kelp forests, rocky intertidal zones, and the Monterey Submarine Canyon (the largest on the West Coast). Graduates have gone on to work at NOAA, the Monterey Bay Aquarium, and National Geographic. "I came here for the dive program," says alum Kalikonani Dailey. "I got a job. I'd recommend it — if you can find the campus through the fog."

📚 Academics (We Have Those)

CSUMB offers 25+ undergraduate degrees, 8 master's programs, and one very specific certificate in "Otter Husbandry" (we made that last one up. Or did we?). With a 25:1 student-to-faculty ratio, your professors will actually know your name — which is great until you skip class.

🧑‍🔬 Service Learning: CSUMB is one of the only universities in America that requires you to do community service to graduate. That's 25+ hours of unpaid labor for the privilege of your diploma. Students call it "voluntold" learning. The community actually benefits, so maybe stop complaining.
DIVING Ongoing

Largest Scientific Dive Program in the Country (Yes, Really)

CSUMB trains 300 divers per year — beating every other university in the US. Students dive weekly in kelp forests, explore the Monterey Submarine Canyon, and graduate to jobs at NOAA, the Monterey Bay Aquarium, and National Geographic. The only downside: you have to get wet. In the Pacific. Which is 55 degrees. You'll be fine. Probably.

TINY CLASSES Always

25:1 Ratio Means Your Professor Knows You Didn't Do the Reading

With small class sizes, there's nowhere to hide. Professors learn your name, your major, your dog's name, and your dog's opinion on the assigned texts. "I can't blend in anymore," says sophomore Jamie L. "Professor Gonzalez asked about my weekend. I panicked and told her the truth. I don't know who I am anymore."

📖 The Library: The Tanimura & Antle Family Memorial Library cost $64 million and is 136,000 square feet of "architectural performance art." You can eat, drink, and nap inside. It's open 24/7 during finals, because the administration knows you procrastinated.

HOUSING CRISIS Fall 2024 — Present

Freshman Enrollment Spikes 43%; Triples Become Quads, Offices Become Dorms

After a surprise enrollment surge, the university discovered that an abandoned Army base does not, in fact, have enough beds. Doubles became triples. Triples became quads. 🍜 Dining (8 Concepts, One Kitchen, Infinite Possibilities)

DINING Fall 2025

Dining Hall Adds Ramen Bar, 8 Food Concepts, and a Ghost Kitchen; Students Still Complain

CONTRACT Perpetuity

Food Vendor Has Right of First Refusal on All Food Entering Campus; Faculty Potlucks Require 3 Approvals

Under every contractor since the school opened — first Sodexo (1997–2021), now Chartwells (operating as "Otter Kitchens") — CSUMB's food vendor has held exclusive catering rights across campus. Faculty who want to bring in outside food — including homemade cookies for a department meeting — must first contact the vendor and ask permission, then submit a Catering Exception Request at least 10 business days in advance (30 for events over 100 people). The form is reviewed by the vendor, then the Corporation, then Risk Management. Each can deny it. "I just wanted to bring a tray of cookies to my own office," said one professor who lived through both regimes. "First I had to ask Sodexo. Now I ask Chartwells. Same form. Same denial. The cookies are still in purgatory."

The policy predates the current vendor by decades. Sodexo ran the Dining Commons and Otter Express from 1997 until Chartwells took over in 2021, rebranding everything as "Otter Kitchens" and renaming the DC to "The Eatery." The right of first refusal clause survived the transition. Other schools have "potlucks." CSUMB has the Catering Exception Request Process — a multi-agency review board for a single casserole. Students have started calling it the "Doctrine of Perpetual Food Grief." One lecturer reportedly tried to bring in a birthday cake for a colleague and was told to "check with the vendor first." The cake remains hypothetical. It has been hypothetical since the Sodexo era.

🍔 Actually Good Stuff: The dining hall serves sustainable seafood from the Monterey Bay Aquarium's partnership program. There's a farmers market on campus. The Library Cafe serves Verve Coffee (from Santa Cruz, actually good). You can buy a $5 compostable to-go container, fill it up, and walk out. They even did birria for orientation — which one student said "made me feel comfortable because that dish is popular in my culture."

MEAL PLANS Every Semester

Meal Plan Pricing Defies All Known Economics

Students can choose from three meal plan tiers: the "Otter" (11 meals/week, $2,145/semester), the "Silver Otter" (14 meals/week, $2,445/semester), and the "Golden Otter Who Clearly Hates Money" (19 meals/week, $2,825/semester). Each plan comes with a declining balance account that loses value faster than a used mattress from East Campus. "I paid $300 for 'Otter Bucks' and the dining hall charged me $14 for a sandwich," says sophomore Emma R. "I did the math. That's $14. For a sandwich. At a school with 8 concepts. I felt each concept individually." The sandwich in question was turkey.

HOURS Perpetually Frustrating

Dining Hall Closes at 8pm; Students Learn What Hunger Is

The Eatery operates on a schedule best described as "hostile to anyone with a late class." Breakfast ends at 10am. Lunch ends at 2pm. Dinner ends at 8pm. On weekends, it closes at 7pm, as if the administration believes hunger takes weekends off. "I had a lab that ended at 7:45pm," says junior marine science major Diego M. "I sprinted across campus. I arrived at 7:58. They were wiping down the Flame station. I watched a man take down the ramen bar in front of my eyes. I haven't been the same since." The only late-night option on campus is a vending machine in the library that sells $4 Kind Bars.

☁️ The Fog Report

Welcome to the only university weather report where "patchy fog" isn't a seasonal phenomenon — it's a lifestyle. CSUMB sits on the Monterey Bay, where the Pacific Ocean meets the coastal range and creates a permanent marine layer that has been known to reduce visibility to "approximately one otter."

🌫️ By the numbers: Monterey experiences "patchy fog" on approximately 180+ days per year. The term "partly cloudy" is used optimistically. The UV index is a theoretical concept. Students from sunnier parts of California have been known to develop Seasonal Affective Disorder within weeks of arrival. We offer free counseling. You'll need it.

🚗 Parking (An Educational Equity Issue)

PARKING 2023 — Present

Students Protest $500 Parking Permits as "Educational Equity Issue"; Permits Now $588

In 2023, 40 students marched demanding free parking, arguing that charging for parking on "stolen Indigenous land" was a racial justice issue. Parking Services responded by raising prices to $588/year, citing "state law." The university also notes that Lot B is "only a 15-minute walk from campus — in good weather. In fog, allow 45 minutes and a prayer."

PARKING Also Always

Parking Permit Prices Defy Inflation, Common Sense

At $588 for an annual pass, CSUMB parking permits cost more than a Monterey Bay Aquarium membership ($95), a wet suit for the dive program ($150), and a year of Verve Coffee at the Library Cafe ($540).
