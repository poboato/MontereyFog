Academics (We Have Those)

CSUMB offers 25+ undergraduate degrees, 8 master's programs, and one very specific certificate in "Otter Husbandry" (we made that last one up. Or did we?). With a 28.6:1 student-to-faculty ratio — recently "optimized" back to 2015 levels — your professors will still know your name. There are just fewer of them now.

🧑‍🔬 Service Learning: CSUMB is one of the only universities in America that requires you to do community service to graduate. That's 25+ hours of unpaid labor for the privilege of your diploma. Students call it "voluntold" learning. The community actually benefits, so maybe stop complaining.
BUDGET 2024 — Present

$12 Million Deficit Solved by Laying Off Staff; $50 Million Science Building Announced

In 2024, CSUMB faced a $12 million structural deficit. The solution: lay off 12 union staff, not renew 4 managers, impose a hiring freeze, and raise the student-to-faculty ratio to 28.6:1. Shortly after, the university announced a brand new $50 million science and engineering building — housing marine science, mechatronics, and NOAA's Monterey Bay National Marine Sanctuary office. "We had to make tough choices," said President Quiñones. "Like whether to fund professors or buildings. We chose the one you can put your name on." Construction begins soon. Classes may be larger, but they'll be larger in a really nice building.

DIVING Ongoing

Largest Scientific Dive Program in the Country (Yes, Really)

CSUMB trains 300 divers per year — beating every other university in the US. Students dive weekly in kelp forests, explore the Monterey Submarine Canyon, and graduate to jobs at NOAA, the Monterey Bay Aquarium, and National Geographic. The only downside: you have to get wet. In the Pacific. Which is 55 degrees. You'll be fine. Probably.

TINY CLASSES Always

25:1 Ratio Means Your Professor Knows You Didn't Do the Reading

With small class sizes, there's nowhere to hide. Professors learn your name, your major, your dog's name, and your dog's opinion on the assigned texts. "I can't blend in anymore," says sophomore Jamie L. "Professor Gonzalez asked about my weekend. I panicked and told her the truth. I don't know who I am anymore."

📖 The Library: The Tanimura & Antle Family Memorial Library cost $64 million and is 136,000 square feet of "architectural performance art." You can eat, drink, and nap inside. It's open 24/7 during finals, because the administration knows you procrastinated.

Notable Programs

MARINE SCIENCE Top Ranked

Marine Science — Because You're Next to the World's Richest Ocean

The Monterey Bay is home to the largest submarine canyon on the West Coast, kelp forests, and otters. Lots of otters. CSUMB's marine science program takes full advantage, with hands-on research, internships at the Monterey Bay Aquarium, and field work that occasionally requires a wet suit and a tolerance for 55°F water.

HCOM Humanities & Communication

HCOM — The Most Confusing Major Name, Explained

HCOM stands for Humanities & Communication. It's CSUMB's interdisciplinary major where you study literature, history, philosophy, and how to write a professional email. Graduates have gone on to become lawyers, teachers, and people who can finally explain to their parents what they studied.

CS + DESIGN Growing Fast

Computer Science — Where the Fog Meets the Cloud

CSUMB's CS program has grown rapidly, with new facilities and partnerships with Silicon Valley and Monterey County tech employers. Students learn everything from algorithms to AI, and graduate to build the systems that will one day predict the fog. (Spoiler: it will always predict fog.)

CHEMISTRY Fall 2023

Nanorobotics Professor Leaves UCLA to Teach at CSUMB; Universe Briefly Confused

Assistant Professor Roshini Ramachandran joined CSUMB in 2023 after six years at UCLA — a move that typically goes in the opposite direction. She studies nanomaterials for defense, medicine, and energy storage, and she chose CSUMB specifically to work closely with undergraduates. "I wanted to be able to run a research lab and mentor students," she said. Translation: she picked a commuter school with no parking and a $12M deficit over a world-class research university. Either CSUMB is that good, or she really hates LA traffic. Possibly both.

🎓 Bottom Line: The academics are solid. The faculty care. The dive program is genuinely world-class. And if you can't find your classroom, just follow the sound of the fog.
